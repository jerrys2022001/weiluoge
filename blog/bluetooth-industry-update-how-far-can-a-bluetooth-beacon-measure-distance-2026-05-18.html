Why does this update matter?

How Far Can a Bluetooth Beacon Measure Distance? matters because it separates the marketing phrase "distance" from the actual engineering job. A beacon usually gives you proximity clues, not a clean tape-measure readout, so the real decision is whether the product needs rough closeness, calibrated zone detection, or a specialist sensing path that can survive the room it is deployed in.

What the signal really says: BeaconZone is useful here because it gives readers a way to separate the headline from the measurement problem. What should teams watch next?

The useful sequence is boring but honest: define the accuracy you actually need, confirm whether the current hardware can meet it, and then see whether the software layer is the bottleneck. If the project only needs a coarse nearby-versus-far signal, keep it simple. If it needs dependable meter-level truth across walls, crowds, or variable mounts, do not let a headline hide the calibration work.
